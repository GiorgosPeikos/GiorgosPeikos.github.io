<div class="card section">
  <h1>DoSSIER (Marie Curie PhD)</h1>

  <p class="muted">
    DoSSIER (Domain Specific Systems for Information Extraction and Retrieval) is an EU Horizon 2020 ITN project on domain specific systems for information extraction and retrieval.
    The programme aims to elucidate, model, and address the information needs of professional users.
    This project received funding from the European Union’s Horizon 2020 research and innovation programme under the Marie Skłodowska Curie grant agreement No 860721.
  </p>

  <p class="muted">
    I was hired as an Early Stage Researcher (ESR) in DoSSIER, where I completed my PhD.
    My research focused on building and evaluating search systems that support professional users in health and legal domains, with emphasis on how users interact with information and how these interactions shape relevance.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="card section">
  <h2>Research outputs</h2>

  <div class="project-grid" style="margin-top:10px;">

    <div class="project-card">
      <div class="item-title">A systematic review of multidimensional relevance estimation in information retrieval</div>
      <div class="muted small">
        Systematic review of 72 studies on how relevance is modelled as multidimensional and dynamic, influenced by user, task, and domain factors.
        Organises relevance aspects, aggregation approaches, and benchmark resources, and highlights how large language models may shape future work via relevance labelling.
      </div>
      <div class="small" style="margin-top:8px;">
        <span class="muted">Takeaway:</span> Relevance modelling benefits from clearer factor definitions and more unified estimation methods across domains.
      </div>
      <div class="project-meta small">
        <span class="muted">Link:</span>
        <a href="https://wires.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/pdf/10.1002%2Fwidm.1541" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer">Wiley (PDF)</a>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="project-card">
      <div class="item-title">Investigating the impact of query representation on medical information retrieval</div>
      <div class="muted small">
        Study on how patient information extracted from clinical notes affects clinical trial allocation and medical literature retrieval.
        Compares rule based and transformer based extraction, disambiguation, negation handling, and concept expansion to form query representations for retrieval.
      </div>
      <div class="small" style="margin-top:8px;">
        <span class="muted">Takeaway:</span> Strong rule based pipelines remain a solid and practical choice for medical retrieval, and careful information selection can matter more than added complexity.
      </div>
      <div class="project-meta small">
        <span class="muted">Link:</span>
        <a href="https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-031-28238-6_42" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer">Springer</a>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="project-card">
      <div class="item-title">Leibi@COLIEE 2022: aggregating tuned lexical models with a cluster driven BERT based model for case law retrieval</div>
      <div class="muted small">
        Submission to COLIEE 2022 case law retrieval: query reformulation for long legal cases, first stage lexical retrieval, neural reranking, and score aggregation.
        Explores statistical extraction methods and embedding based strategies, plus a cluster driven reranking approach and linear score aggregation.
      </div>
      <div class="small" style="margin-top:8px;">
        <span class="muted">Takeaway:</span> Information extraction and compression before retrieval is essential for long legal documents, and strong statistical approaches provide robust gains.
      </div>
      <div class="project-meta small">
        <span class="muted">Link:</span>
        <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.13351" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer">arXiv</a>
      </div>
    </div>
